## What I explored

Three related experiments over about six weeks:

**Quantized inference.** Running Mistral 7B at 4-bit and 8-bit quantization via llama.cpp and Ollama. Benchmarking against full-precision on summarization, classification, and code generation.

**LoRA fine-tuning.** Fine-tuning Mistral 7B on a small domain-specific corpus using PEFT and bitsandbytes. Training time on an RTX 3090 was around 3 hours per 1,000 steps at batch size 4.

**Prompt engineering vs. fine-tuning.** Comparing structured prompt templates against LoRA-adapted models on the same evaluation set to understand when fine-tuning is actually worth the effort.

<Callout type="insight">
For structured output tasks where the output format is constrained and the domain is narrow, well-engineered prompts often match a LoRA-adapted model — and take minutes, not hours, to iterate on.
</Callout>

## Next Steps

The natural next experiment is distillation: using a large teacher model to generate training data for a smaller student, then fine-tuning the student with LoRA. This could close more of the quality gap at lower inference cost.
